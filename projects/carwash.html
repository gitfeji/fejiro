Spot Free Car Wash

A frustrating trip to a self-serve car wash led to a full UX investigation — and a mobile app that makes the whole experience frictionless, even from your wrist.

Spot Free Car Wash
UX/UI Designer (Solo)
User Observation · ERAF Diagram · Prototyping
2023
View in Figma ↗
Three core screens — Connect via QR, Real-time monitoring, Account balance

The Problem

Self-serve car washes seem simple — pull in, pay, wash, leave. But when you actually observe people using them, the friction is everywhere. Machines go down with no notice. Users don't know their account balance until they're already mid-wash. There's no easy way to report a broken bay. Staff are unreachable.

I went to Spot Free Car Wash with a notebook and just watched. I tracked user behaviour, recorded inefficiencies, and mapped the full service flow using an ERAF diagram (Entities, Relations, Attributes, Flows). What I found was a service that worked despite its interface, not because of it.

Key Challenges

Challenge 01

No communication channel between users and maintenance staff. Issues went unreported or took too long to resolve.

Challenge 02

Account balance was invisible until users were already committed to a wash cycle.

Challenge 03

No way to see which bays were available or operational before pulling in.

Challenge 04

The physical setup demanded both hands — no hands-free option for timing or controlling wash functions.

The Solution

I designed a mobile app that connects users to car wash facilities via QR code scanning — scan the bay, and you're connected instantly. From there, users get real-time monitoring of their wash cycle, account balance visibility at all times, and a direct line to report equipment issues.

Home screen — balance, nearby facilities, and active session at a glance

The standout feature is Apple Watch integration. Once connected to a bay, users can monitor and control their wash from their wrist — no need to hold their phone while managing hoses and equipment. It's a small detail that makes a real difference when your hands are full.

Facilities view — bay status, availability, and distance

Outcomes

QR

Instant bay connection — no account setup friction

⌚

Apple Watch integration for hands-free control

RT

Real-time monitoring and issue reporting

The app transformed a passive, confusing experience into an active, informed one. Users always know what's happening, what it's costing, and who to contact if something goes wrong.

Reflection

This project was a lesson in the power of field observation. I could have designed an app based on assumptions about what car wash users want — but spending time actually watching people use the service revealed problems I never would have anticipated from a desk. The Apple Watch feature, for example, came directly from watching people juggle their phone with wet, soapy hands.

It also reinforced my belief that the best UX solutions are specific. Not a generic "service app" — a solution designed for exactly this context, this user, this moment.
