UX Audit & Redesign · 2023

Fanduel Redesign

A structured usability audit of one of North America's biggest sports betting apps — revealing where the experience breaks down, and designing a cleaner path forward.

Fanduel (Academic Project)
UX/UI Designer (Solo)
App Audit · Task Analysis · Redesign
2023
Redesigned Fanduel screens — Home, Sport Selection, Betslip, and Cashout

Context

Fanduel is one of the leading sports betting platforms in North America, with millions of users placing bets across basketball, football, hockey, and more. Sports betting apps carry a unique UX challenge: the audience ranges from first-time casual bettors to seasoned daily users, and the stakes of a confusing interface are literally financial.

I conducted this audit as part of an inclusive design course, with a focus on surfacing usability barriers that affect a broad range of users — not just power users who've learned to navigate around the app's quirks.

The Audit

I gave users a simple task: place a $1 bet on a specific game scenario, then delete the selection. Watching people attempt this revealed friction at every stage.

Issue 01

Promotions and games looked identical in the navigation — users couldn't tell what was a bet and what was an ad.

Issue 02

Account balance was only visible on certain screens, forcing users to backtrack constantly to check funds.

Issue 03

Deleting a bet selection required navigating to the betslip through multiple steps — with no clear indicator of how many active bets existed.

Issue 04

Live betting was buried — a high-value feature for returning users that was nearly impossible to discover.

The Redesign

I developed a targeted redesign focused on the core betting flow — from landing on the home screen to completing a bet and managing the betslip.

Redesigned home screen — clear sport navigation, promoted live bets, balance always visible

Key changes made:

  • Navigation bar — reorganised to feature sport leagues and favourites, with promos clearly separated from games
  • Balance visibility — account balance now persists across all screens in the header
  • Betslip badge — notification count on the betslip icon tells users at a glance how many active bets they have
  • Sport selection — users can customise their home screen by picking favourite sports during onboarding, reflected immediately in the nav
  • Live bet notifications — push to lock screen so returning users never miss a live betting window
Sport selection onboarding — personalises the home navigation immediately

Reflection

What struck me most about this project was how a product used by millions of people could have such clear, fixable usability gaps. The core issue wasn't that Fanduel's team hadn't thought about UX — it's that complex products accumulate friction over time, especially when new features are added without revisiting the foundational flows.

This project sharpened my instinct for critical evaluation. I learned to look past familiarity ("it works because I know how to use it") and focus on the experience of someone encountering the app for the first time.
